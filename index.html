Art Talks

Art Talks is a series of evening events for women, intended to heighten awareness, interest and knowledge about art in its many forms. At each event, a short talk is presented by an expert from the art world in a setting that encourages (facilitates) learning, socializing with friends and the enjoyment of refreshments (beverages and snacks).

Art Talks is the brainchild of nine like-minded Calgary women who believe in the importance of education and whose vision is to support the educational success of urban aboriginal girls through a partnership with the Rotary Club’s Tom Jackson Stay in School (RTJSIS) program. Each event is intended to raise money so that these girls may participate in the RTJSIS program and receive support through grades 6-12 (up to $1,500/year, $10,500 in total).

THE ROTARY TOM JACKSON STAY IN SCHOOL PROGRAM (RTJSIS)

The mission of the RTJSIS is to positively impact aboriginal girls’ high school graduation rates through mentoring, tutoring and financial support as needed. It is designed to encourage ‘at risk’ students to continue with their education.

The program targets two high need areas of Calgary and provides financial assistance to selected aboriginal students as a kick start to high school graduation and post secondary education.
